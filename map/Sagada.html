Sagada - Interesting Coffins

Journey to Sagada to experience the awe-inspiring sight of coffins suspended on cliff faces, a testament to the enduring spiritual beliefs and respect for ancestors held by the local community.

Why Sagada is Special to Me

                                                      Sagada holds a special place in my heart because of its unique blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. The sight of the hanging coffins, a practice deeply rooted in the Igorot tradition, is both humbling and fascinating. The serene mountain landscapes, the cool climate, and the warm hospitality of the locals create an unforgettable experience. The quiet and peaceful atmosphere allows for deep reflection and a connection with nature. Exploring the caves and trekking through the lush forests feels like stepping into a different world, far removed from the hustle and bustle of city life. The respect shown to ancestors and the preservation of age-old traditions make Sagada a place of profound significance.

How to Get There

Getting to Sagada usually involves a long but scenic bus ride from Manila. Here's a general guide:

  • From Manila: Take a bus from Cubao or Pasay to Banaue (approximately 9-10 hours).
  • From Banaue: Take a jeepney or bus to Sagada (approximately 2-3 hours).
  • Alternatively, you can take a direct bus from Manila to Sagada, which may be more convenient.
It's recommended to check bus schedules and book tickets in advance, especially during peak seasons.

Other Places to Visit in Sagada/Philippines

While in Sagada, consider visiting these other attractions:

  • Sumaguing Cave: Known for its stunning rock formations and adventurous spelunking experience.
  • Bomod-ok Falls: A beautiful waterfall requiring a scenic trek.
  • Kiltepan Viewpoint: Famous for its breathtaking sunrise views.
  • Lake Danum: A serene lake perfect for relaxation and reflection.
  • Marlboro Country: Offers stunning mountain views and hiking trails.
If you have more time in the Philippines, explore other destinations like:
  • Banaue Rice Terraces: A UNESCO World Heritage Site.
  • Palawan: Known for its pristine beaches and stunning lagoons.
  • Boracay: Famous for its white sand beaches and vibrant nightlife.
  • Cebu: Offers a mix of historical sites, beautiful beaches, and adventure activities.
